What‚Äôs a Power Distribution Box (Power Distro)?

A power distro is a piece of gear that takes in high-capacity electrical power (like 240V or 208V three-phase), and safely breaks it down into multiple circuits ‚Äî usually standard 120V or 240V outputs ‚Äî with circuit breakers, monitoring, and connectors suitable for pro audio gear.

üß± Think of it like:

A heavy-duty power splitter + breaker box + surge protection, designed for concerts, festivals, clubs, or large A/V setups.

üõ†Ô∏è Why You Need One

If your amp(s) can output 13,600W (like your 4√ó3400W setup), then:

  • You can‚Äôt run all that off one wall outlet.
  • A power distro allows you to:
    • Pull high current safely from a big power source.
    • Split it into multiple 15A, 20A, or 30A circuits.
    • Protect each circuit with breakers.
    • Monitor voltage and current in real time.
    • Use pro-grade connectors like PowerCON, L6-30, or Edison.

üîå Types of Inputs/Outputs on a Power Distro

üî≤ Inputs:

  • Cam-Lok (for 100A+ 3-phase hookups at venues)
  • L14-30 or L21-30 (twist-lock, 30A/240V or 208V)
  • Hardwire terminals (for permanent installs)

üî≥ Outputs:

  • Standard Edison (NEMA 5-15) 15A/120V outlets
  • L5-20 or L6-30 twist-lock 120V/240V
  • PowerCON or True1 connectors for pro audio gear

üéõÔ∏è Example Use Case

Imagine you're running:

  • 2x amps, each pulling ~30A at 120V
  • 1x DSP rack or mixer, ~5A
  • A few lights or fans

You could connect a 50A 240V line to a power distro, then:

  • Break it down into multiple 20A 120V circuits
  • Feed each amp on its own protected circuit
  • Monitor load to avoid tripping breakers

üèÅ Popular Power Distro Brands

  • Furman (makes portable rackmount models)
  • LEX Products
  • Motion Labs
  • PowerWerks
  • ProX, AC Power, Middle Atlantic (rack-mount and budget options)

‚ö†Ô∏è Caution

Power distros deal with dangerous levels of current ‚Äî get a licensed electrician if you're working with anything over a regular outlet.

For small setups, sometimes you can get by with multiple wall circuits ‚Äî but once you‚Äôre above ~3000‚Äì4000W, a distro or 240V setup is safer and cleaner.

Goal

Soundsystem that is:
  • Extremely Powerful
  • Portable
  • Efficient
  • Expandable
  • "Home" or "Unprepared" Grade Power Requirements
  • "Affordable"
While audio power can be achieved with many speakers, this may not be very portable (or easy to store).
Also, while audio power can be achieved with less speakers by supplying more power to the speakers, this may not be feasible with the available non-commercial electricity.
Therefore, need a speaker design that can provide the most power for the least amount of drivers and efficient amplifiers (minimal heat loss).
Also, it would be useful to have a system that could be expanded and added onto over time. A passive speaker solution with central amplifier allows this easily and allows for easier powering and control from a central unit.
A passive speaker setup also allows for separation of the "hardcore" electronics (amplifier) from the more resistant speaker boxes (solid cabinets with speakers and speaker wire to a jack panel).
While amplifiers are better left to the pros, rigging speakers and cables to a jack panel in a wood enclosure is a realistic DIY project.
However, I would want to avoid just making any rough or improvised cab and experimenting through trial and error - a well proven design will already have the efficiency people count on from the off the shelf product.
