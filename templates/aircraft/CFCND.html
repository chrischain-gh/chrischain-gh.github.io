Emergeny Procedures

Electrical Fire in Flight
  1. Master Switch – Off
  2. Avionics Master Switch – Off
  3. Electrical Switches – Off
  4. Vents/Cabin Air – Closed/Off
  5. Fire Extinguisher – Activate
  6. Cabin – Ventilate
  7. If fire is out and power is needed...
Engine Fire During Start
  1. Cranking – Continue with starter engaged
  2. If engine starts:
    1. Throttle – 1700 RPM for 3 minutes
    2. Engine – Shutdown/Inspect
  3. If engine fails to start:
    1. Throttle – Full Open
    2. Mixture – Idle Cutoff
    3. Cranking – Continue
    4. Fire Extinguisher – Obtain
    5. Master Switch – Off
    6. Ignition Switch – Off
    7. Fuel Selector – Off
    8. Fire – Extinguish
    9. Fire Damage – Inspect
Wing Fire
  1. Landing Lights – Off
  2. Navigation Lights – Off
  3. Perform Sideslip to keep flames away from fuel
  4. Land As Soon As Possible
Cabin Fire
  1. Master Switch – Off
  2. Vents/Cabin Air – Closed/Off
  3. Fire Extinguisher – Activate
  4. Cabin – Ventilate
  5. Land As Soon As Possible
Precautionary Landing w/ Engine
  1. Seats/Seatbelts – Secure
  2. Flaps – 20°
  3. Airspeed – 65–70 MPH
  4. Landing Site – Overfly & Assess
  5. Electrical Switches – Off
  6. Avionics Master – Off
  7. Master Switch – Off
  8. Flaps – 30–40° on Final Approach
  9. Doors – Unlatch
  10. Touchdown – Nose High
  11. Ignition Key – Off
  12. Brakes – Apply Heavily
Emergency Landing w/o Engine:
  1. Radio – “MAYDAY” on ATC or 121.5
  2. Transponder – 7700
  3. Airspeed – 70 MPH Flaps UP
  4. 60 MPH Flaps DOWN
  5. Mixture – Idle Cutoff
  6. Fuel Selector – Off
  7. Ignition Switch – Off
  8. Flaps – As Required
  9. Master Switch – Off
  10. Doors – Unlatch
  11. Touchdown – Tail Low
  12. Brakes – Apply Heavily
Engine Fire During Flight:
  1. Mixture – Idle Cutoff
  2. Fuel Selector – Off
  3. Master Switch – Off
  4. Cabin Heat/Air – Off
  5. Airspeed – 110 MPH or more
  6. Master Landing – Execute
  • Emergency Frequency:
    1. 121.5
    2. Emergency Transponder Code – 7700
    3. Radio Failure/Transponder Code – 7600
    Carbon Monoxide Detector Alarm
    1. Shut off (push) cabin heat
    2. Shut off (pull) cabin air knob on panel
    3. Open wing root vents
    4. Open window in left cabin door
    Ammeter Shows Excessive Charge
    1. Alternator CB – Pull
    2. Nonessential Electrical Equip – Off
    3. Land As Soon As Practical
    Ammeter Shows Excessive Discharge
    1. Avionics Master – Off
    2. Alternator CB – Check In
    3. Master Switch – Off
    4. Master Switch – On
    5. Ammeter – Check if Charging
    6. Avionics Master – On
    7. If Discharge continues:
    8. Non-essential Elec Equip – Off
    9. Land As Soon As Practical

    Aux Fuel Tank

    Unclear if it's stock (because no baggage door and was at one point a factory option for other Cessnas) or add on/STC (closest I found was http://www.griggsaircraft.com/fabrication/aux_fuel_systems but no C175 and slight details don't match)

    Cessna Aux Fuel Tank

    Placard reads: "Place pump OFF before changing FROM LEFT tank"
    1. Takeoff, climb, and land with fuel selector valve handle set in "BOTH ON" position
    2. At cruise altitude, switch to "RIGHT TANK" position and operate from this fuel tank until the fuel supply is exhausted
    3. Switch to "LEFT TANK" for operation
    4. Pull on aux fuel pump switch and refill right main fuel tank from aux fuel tank
    5. WATCH THE MAIN TANK FUEL LEVEL CLOSELY, DO NOT OVERFILL
    6. Push aux fuel pump switch to "OFF" when fuel transfer is completed (transfer of fuel will take 45-60 minutes TBC!!!)
    7. DO NOT OPERATE THE AUX FUEL PUMP WITH THE FUEL SELECTOR ON "BOTH" OR "RIGHT" AFTER FUEL TRANSFER HAS BEEN COMPLETED - ENGINE STOPPAGE WILL OCCUR, IF IT HAPPENS SWITCH PUMP OFF AND WAIT 3-5 SECONDS
    8. Return fuel selector valve handle to "BOTH ON" or even "RIGHT" position after refilling the right tank and shutting off the aux fuel pump
